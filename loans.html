Predicting Loan Default Rates with Alternative Data

This was the final project for a course in Business Analytics I took while at the University of Virginia. Data were collected from the Urban Institute and a major credit bureau containing more than 5 million records. A two-step cleaning approach was taken (Excel and R were both used to ensure identical results).

Exploratory analysis and model creation was done in R. While multiple predictice analytics models were built, it was concluded that a linear model with median medical debt in collections, share with student loan debt in default, auto/retail loan delinquency rate and share of people in rural areas as coefficients created the best model. As with any academic project, I'd strongly recommend checking out the whole paper.
